Dermaplaning (A, C, AS)

Also called Epidermal Leveling or Blading, is a safe, effective form of manual exfoliation, using a sterile surgical blade. It represents the latest in skin rejuvenation techniques.

Epidermal Leveling safely removes the top layers of dead skin cells, leaving the skin immediately suppler, smoother and more vibrant. In addition, the procedure removes the very fine vellus (“baby” or “peach fuzz”) hairs that waxing often misses.

The process reduces acne scarring, uneven pigmentation and fine lines, is suitable for both men and women, and can be performed on the guest’s first visit. It is also effective in treating rosacea and pre-cancerous cells. Most people notice marked improvements in the texture and appearance of their skin immediately, making it an ideal “special event” procedure.

Epidermal Leveling can be performed alone, or in conjunction with other treatments, such as chemical peels or laser rejuvenation procedures. The Epidermal Leveling process is safe for pregnant or nursing patients. It is also an effective form of facial depilation for guests who are contraindicated for waxing.

This hands-on class provides professional training, which is essential to performing the procedure safely and effectively. Attendees will also receive Epidermal Leveling tools, and a certificate.

*Tools: Blades, Blade Handle, Blade Remover, and handouts.

Cancellation Policy:


Because of demand and limited number of seats available in our workshops, cancellations made 7 or more days prior to the start of a workshop may have their non-refundable registration fee moved to another workshop. If requested, cancellations made less that seven days prior to the workshop will be allowed a 100% refund minus a $150 non-refundable deposit. “No-Shows” and cancellations made less than 24 hours prior to the start of the workshop will forfeit the full tuition paid. Workshops under $50 have a $20 deposit. Workshops that cost more than $50 have a $50 deposit, except for the Lash Extension Workshop, which is a $150 deposit. Payments may be made by cash, check, Visa, MasterCard, or Discover. Full payment is due one business day prior to date of workshop, except for the Lash Extension Workshop, which is due one week before the workshop date.
